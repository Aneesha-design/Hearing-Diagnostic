Audiogram Test

This audiogram exam will help find out if you may have hearing loss and help to tell you the extent of your hearing loss. Before you begin, follow the instructions listed below:

1. This exam must be used with headphones in order to detect changes in the left/right ear. Use the highest quality of headphones that you have to take this test.

2. Before we begin, we are going to calibrate your device to an appropriate sound level. To do this, we are going to simulate a 40 dB sound. Without headphones, rub your hands together closely in front of your nose. Observe the volume of sound produced. Then, with your headphones on, click the button below. Adjust the sound level of your device so that both sound levels match (hands rubbing without headphones, and button audio with headphones.



3. Try to take this test in a quiet environment as possible for optimal results.

What does this chart tell me?

This chart is called an audiogram. The frequencies, or pitches of sound you can hear are measured on the horizontal axis, while the volume of sound you can hear are measured on the vertical axis. Generally speaking, the volume level you hear should be consistently between -10 and 30 dB (decibels) of volume. However, as you age, this level will increase for higher frequencies.


Things you should look out for:


- a drastic difference among your hearing in the left and right ears - a line that is consistently above 30 dB for all frequencies - a line that slopes towards a decibel level above 30 dB for high frequencies (2000+ Hz)
